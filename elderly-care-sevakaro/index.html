PROBLEM

India has 172 million elderly. Almost none have organised care.

Three irreversible shifts have created a crisis families can no longer solve alone — and no organised business has reached Hooghly district.

Elderly care at your doorstep

👨‍👩‍👧

Joint families collapsed

Urban joint families: 37% (2008) → under 5% today. 70%+ households are nuclear. Elderly parents home alone — managing medicines, doctors, emergencies by themselves.

✈️

Children moved far away

35.4 million Indians live abroad. Millions more in distant cities. They send money, call daily — but money cannot take Baba to the doctor at 6 AM.

💊

Chronic disease is permanent

75% of elderly manage ≥1 chronic condition — hypertension, diabetes, cataracts. 27% have multiple. They need daily support, not occasional visits.

The daily reality in Bandel: An elderly woman wakes at 5:30 AM. No one to help with medicines. No one to fetch fresh fish from the bazar. No one to accompany her to the cardiologist. Her child in Bengaluru gets the call only when something goes wrong. This is the reality for 660,000+ elderly in Hooghly — and zero organised providers serve them.

PROBLEM

Supply is almost zero. Demand is massive and accelerating.

Five exploitable gaps define SevaKaro's position. Together they create a moat that metro players cannot replicate cheaply.

GEOGRAPHY GAP

Metros only — Tier 3 invisible

Emoha, Portea, Anvayaa — all in metros. Hooghly (660K+ elderly) has zero organised elder care. This is the primary whitespace.

PRICE GAP

Missing middle has no options

Emoha ₹2,999/mo. Anvayaa ₹6,999/mo. Middle-class elderly — the largest group — cannot afford these. No one offers real service below ₹2,000/mo.

CHANNEL GAP

Apps elders cannot use

85% of elderly are digitally illiterate. Yet every competitor leads with an app. No one built a WhatsApp-native, Bengali-language service system.

BREADTH GAP

Specialists, no generalists

Portea = health. Emoha = relationships. No affordable player covers daily errands + health + meals + admin + emergency together in small towns.

TRUST GAP

Inconsistent quality industry-wide

Industry complaint: untrained caregivers, inconsistent service. Emoha's 110+ city franchise creates quality variance. A locally-embedded COCO model wins on trust.

SevaKaro's position

₹600–1,500/mo · WhatsApp-first · Bengali-native · Broad services · COCO quality · Hooghly — zero competitors.

Only 10% of elderly are aware paid care services exist. The first player to create awareness in Hooghly captures the market. Awareness-building IS a competitive advantage.

OPERATIONS

A para woman with a contract. ₹700 margin per meals home.

SevaKaro charges subscribers ₹2,200/mo for meals. Pays contractor ₹1,500/mo. The ₹700 difference is pure margin. The real value: Bengali food, cooked right, by someone from the para.

How the contractor model works

Who to recruit
A local para woman, 35–55, known to cook well. SHG members ideal. She already cooks at home — this is 2–3 extra portions daily. She earns ₹1,500 guaranteed per home/mo.
The contract
Written agreement: ₹1,500/home/mo, tiffin by 12 PM and 7 PM, rotating menu based on elder preferences, SevaKaro hygiene standards, 3-day notice if unwell.
The menu philosophy
Bengali elderly identity IS food. Ilish in mustard in season. Rui macher jhol on regular days. Luchi on Sunday. Nolen gur payesh in winter. Document each elder's preferences.
Quality control
Zone worker does spot checks. Families give WhatsApp feedback. Monthly review. Escalation: warn → warn → replace. Always have 1 backup identified.

How do you know it's working? Three signals don't lie.

PMF for elder care is different from consumer apps. It is slower, more emotional, and built on word-of-mouth. Watch these signals in the first 90 days.

😊

Signal 1: NPS 60+

Ask each subscriber family every 3 months: How likely to recommend SevaKaro (1–10)? NPS 60+ = PMF. Below 40 = something is fundamentally broken.

🔄

Signal 2: Referrals coming in

Are families referring neighbours unprompted? In Bengali para culture, word travels fast. If 1 in 5 new subscribers comes via word-of-mouth, PMF is building.

💬

Signal 3: 'Devastated if gone'

Ask: How would you feel if SevaKaro shut down tomorrow? If 40%+ say 'Very disappointed' — you have PMF. This is the Sean Ellis test.

STRUCTURE

Seven risks. All manageable. None fatal.

Understanding risks in advance is half the solution. Every risk below has a specific mitigation — not a vague "manage carefully" answer.

👷Worker leaves suddenly
MEDIUM probability HIGH impact
Always identify 1–2 backup candidates before you need them. Build subscriber relationship directly with the brand — not the individual worker — so families stay even if the worker changes. Part-time helper covers the gap during transition.
🤝Subscriber trusts worker more than brand
HIGH probability MEDIUM impact
Founder calls every subscriber personally once a month — the relationship must be with SevaKaro, not one person. Monthly NPS survey sent directly from founder. Worker rotation policy after 12 months in the same home.
🍱Food contractor quality drops
LOW-MED probability MEDIUM impact
Monthly quality audit with family feedback. 3-strike policy: warn → formal warning → replace. Always maintain 1 backup contractor identified per zone before you need one.
🙏Cultural trust barrier — outsourcing parental care
MEDIUM probability MEDIUM impact
Frame as supplementary, not replacement. 7-day free trial removes all risk. One doctor endorsement creates massive trust transfer. Neighbour/para testimonials carry more weight than any ad.
🚨Emergency: subscriber has a health crisis
MEDIUM probability HIGH impact
108 ambulance (free in WB) pre-saved in all worker phones. Hospital route mapped per subscriber. Emergency contacts accessible offline. Zone worker drilled quarterly on response protocol. This moment, handled well, becomes your strongest referral story.
📈Slow subscriber growth
LOW-MED probability MEDIUM impact
Double down on doctor + chemist partnerships. Run a free health camp. Add a referral cash bonus (₹500 per family referred). Growth is slow only when product-market fit isn't confirmed — return to trial-first approach.
🏢Large competitor enters the market
LOW probability (3 yrs) HIGH if it happens
Your moat is local trust, not technology. A metro player entering Bandel takes 18–24 months to establish community credibility. You'll be 3 years ahead. Expand quickly to lock in the best zone workers before competitors recruit them.

Every zone worker is a SevaKaro employee. Every payment goes to SevaKaro UPI — never directly to the worker. Fixed salary, not commission. This eliminates side-deals, undercutting, and service inconsistency.

Community, not hierarchy

Weekly ESS WhatsApp group — share wins, tips, difficult situations. Peer learning. The best ESS soldiers train newcomers and earn a mentorship bonus.

Performance issues trigger a support conversation, not a warning letter. Identify root cause — personal problem, skill gap, zone mismatch. Fix first. Part ways only if needed.
