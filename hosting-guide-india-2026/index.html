The India Tax Landscape for Cloud Payments

Four tax/cost layers apply when an Indian startup pays for foreign cloud services. Understanding each one determines your real monthly spend.

Layer 1 — Mandatory

GST via Reverse Charge (RCM)

18%

When an Indian business pays a foreign SaaS/cloud vendor with no PE in India, it's classified as an "import of service" under IGST Act. You — the buyer — must self-assess 18% IGST, pay it to government via GSTR-3B, and file monthly.

The foreign vendor's invoice will typically show no GST. You are still legally obligated to pay it under RCM.

✓ Claimable as ITC if GST-registered business
Layer 2 — If Claimable

Input Tax Credit (ITC)

−18%

If you are GST registered and using these cloud services for your taxable business outputs (SaaS products, API services etc.), you can claim back 100% of the RCM GST paid as Input Tax Credit in your next GSTR-3B filing.

Net effective GST cost = ₹0 for a properly registered startup. But you must front the cash and wait for next month's offset.

✓ Net zero cost — just a cash flow timing issue
Layer 3 — Payment Cost

Forex Markup on International Cards

1–3.5%

Most Indian bank credit cards charge a forex markup of 1.5–3.5% on international transactions (HDFC Regalia: 2%, SBI Prime: 3.5%, etc.). Some cards like Niyo Global, IDFC First Wealth, IndusInd Pioneer charge 0% markup.

At ₹25,000/month hosting spend, 3.5% markup = ₹875 extra per month = ₹10,500/year wasted.

ℹ Use zero-markup card to eliminate this cost
Layer 4 — Above Threshold

TCS under LRS (above ₹10 Lakh/year)

5–20%

TCS applies on outward remittances above ₹10 lakh in a financial year (Budget 2025 raised threshold from ₹7L to ₹10L). For most cloud hosting via international credit cards, TCS is currently deferred (Ministry of Finance postponed applicability on intl credit card spends).

At $300/month × ₹85 = ₹3,06,000/year — well under the ₹10L LRS threshold. TCS not triggered for most startup budgets.

✓ Not triggered at $100–500/mo budget
ℹ️
Budget 2026 Bonus: India's Union Budget 2026–27 proposes a tax holiday until 2047 for foreign cloud companies that route services through Indian data centers. This means AWS (Mumbai), GCP (Mumbai/Delhi), and Azure (Pune) regions will likely see lower prices and more capacity over time as global providers expand Indian infrastructure aggressively. Using Indian regions of these clouds avoids cross-border remittance complexity entirely.

GST & ITC Strategy for Your Startup

How to ensure the 18% GST on all your cloud spending costs you absolutely nothing in practice.

1

Register for GST Immediately

Register your startup under GST if you haven't. Services exports and B2B SaaS both require it. Registration is free, takes 3–5 working days online at gstin.gov.in. Your GSTIN unlocks ITC claims on all foreign cloud spend.

gstin.gov.in — free
2

File GST RCM in GSTR-3B Monthly

Each month, self-assess 18% IGST on all foreign cloud invoices (converted to INR at the rate on invoice date). Pay this in Table 3.1(d) of GSTR-3B. You can simultaneously claim it as ITC in Table 4(A)(3) — same return, net effect is zero.

Monthly — same day pay & claim
3

Add Your GSTIN to All Cloud Accounts

AWS, GCP, and Azure have GSTIN fields in billing settings — add yours and request GST-compliant invoices from these platforms (they have Indian entities). For Railway/Vercel/Modal, keep the foreign invoices as proof for RCM calculation.

AWS India billing: GSTIN supported
4

If Exporting SaaS: File LUT for Zero-Rate

If your products are sold to customers outside India (charging in USD), your outward supplies are zero-rated exports. File a Letter of Undertaking (LUT) each year — you pay zero GST on your sales AND can claim ITC refunds on your cloud costs. This is the most tax-efficient structure.

Zero-rated exports — no output GST
5

If Selling to Indian B2B Customers

Charge 18% IGST on invoices. Your Indian business customers can claim it back as ITC. Cloud costs (GST paid via RCM) offset your output GST liability — net payable often zero or near-zero at startup scale. Win-win with customers.

B2B chain: ITC flows both ways
6

Hire a CA Familiar with Tech Startups

GST compliance for foreign SaaS payments, RCM, OIDAR services, and LUT filings is specialized. A good CA who knows tech/SaaS costs ₹3,000–8,000/month and saves you far more in penalties and missed ITC claims. Cities like Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune have many SaaS-savvy CAs.

₹3,000–8,000/mo — worth every rupee

India-Specific Money-Saving Tips

1

Use GCP Mumbai for Indian Users, Railway for Global

If your products serve Indian users (B2C apps, consumer SaaS), GCP Mumbai region with INR billing + proper GST invoice is better than Railway + Singapore region. Combine: Railway for backend logic, GCP Cloud SQL Mumbai for database if Indian latency is critical.

10ms Indian latency on GCP Mumbai
2

DPIIT Startup Registration = More Perks

Register under DPIIT Startup India scheme (free). This unlocks: AWS Activate India partner credits, GCP India startup program credits, Azure for Startups credits — all offered through domestic India channels which may have different (better) credit amounts than global programs.

startupindia.gov.in — free
3

Pay Annually for 10–20% Discount

Railway, Vercel, and Supabase all offer discounts for annual billing. Combined with the forex rate lock-in, paying annually saves 10–20%. At ₹25,000/month that's ₹30,000–60,000 annual savings. Budget from savings or early revenue to take this.

Save ₹30K–60K/year
4

Stripe India for Payment Collection

If your SaaS products charge customers, use Stripe India (activated via Stripe Atlas or direct). Stripe India sends GST-compliant invoices to Indian customers, handles RBI FEMA compliance for foreign customers, and deposits INR to your Indian bank account.

Stripe India — RBI regulated
5

Razorpay for Indian Customers

For any product with Indian B2B or B2C customers, use Razorpay (not Stripe). Lower domestic transaction fees, UPI support, instant INR settlement, GST-compliant invoices, and no currency conversion cost. International customers can still use Stripe.

~2% domestic vs 2.9%+$0.30 Stripe
6

Budget for ₹90/$ in Your Projections

The rupee has depreciated consistently. Plan your unit economics assuming ₹88–90/$ for cloud costs. If the rate stays at ₹85, you get a pleasant buffer. If it weakens, you're covered. Lock in annual billing when USD is at a relatively favorable rate.

Plan at ₹90/$ for safety margin

Monthly by 20th of following month
