Section 06 — Health Comparison

Dhunuchi vs room fresheners: the honest truth

Neither is perfectly clean. But only one has 2,000 years of biological data behind it — and a clear technology path to near-zero harm.

✓
The key insight: exposure pattern, not peak values

During a dhunuchi burn, PM2.5 spikes — but this lasts 15–20 minutes, 4–5× per year. A plug-in freshener runs 24/7, delivering phthalates (found in 86% of fresheners, per NRDC) continuously for weeks. Total annual chemical load from dhunuchi is far lower.

Section 07 — The Science (Plain English)

Why it actually works — the biology behind the claims

You don't need to understand the chemistry to run the business. But investors and regulators respect founders who do. Here's the simple version.

🔬
Business significance of the science

The antimicrobial and repellent properties of dhuno resin are documented in peer-reviewed journals including Nature Communications (2026) and the Journal of Ethnopharmacology. This means a competitor cannot simply dispute the claims — the science is settled. It also means marketing can reference "traditional Indian wisdom, now validated by modern science" — the most powerful brand story in the wellness category.

What makes dhuno special — 4 key molecules

Camphor
The repellent
Sublimes at room temperature. Activates pain receptors in mosquitoes and lizards — they physically flee from it. 97.6% repellency against malaria-carrying Anopheles mosquitoes.
Borneol
The anti-mosquito key
2026 Nature study: activates OR49 receptor in mosquito's smell organ, overriding its ability to detect human body odour. Essentially makes you invisible to mosquitoes.
α-Pinene
The antibacterial
Makes up 68.6% of resin smoke. Punches holes in bacterial cell walls, killing them directly. Responsible for the 91% airborne bacteria reduction in studies.
Incensole acetate
The calming compound
Forms when resin burns. Crosses the blood-brain barrier and activates TRPV3 channels — producing measurable anxiolytic (anti-anxiety) effects. The molecule behind "the incense calms you."

The key engineering insight: temperature controls everything

Electric warming at 80–120°C releases all the good compounds without any combustion. The problem (smoke, PM2.5, benzene) only starts above 300°C. Green = beneficial aromatics. Red = harmful emissions.

✓
Electric dhunuchi = all the benefit, near-zero harm

At 80–120°C, beneficial compounds (borneol, camphor, α-pinene) vaporise completely. Harmful combustion products (PM2.5, benzene, PAHs) only form above 300°C. An electric dhunuchi is medically superior to a plug-in freshener — this is the core product story and the regulatory safe harbour.

Section 08 — Regulatory Compliance

What the law requires — and what it allows

Good news: the regulatory path is clear, the requirements are reasonable, and India's new BIS standard (December 2025) actually rewards clean, natural products like yours.

✓
New BIS IS 19412:2025 — advantage for premium natural brands

Released on National Consumer Day, December 26, 2025, India's first dedicated incense standard bans harmful insecticides (alethrin, permethrin, fipronil) and synthetic fragrance chemicals that most mass-market competitors use. A natural dhuno brand is already compliant by design — while competitors have to reformulate. This is a competitive advantage, not a burden.

Compliance roadmap — 14 months to full export readiness

Month 1–2
UDYAM MSME + GST + Trademark registration. Total cost: ~₹5–8K. All free or nearly free.
Month 2–4
BIS IS 19412:2025 compliance testing via CSIR-IITR or CFTRI. Apply for BIS Standard Mark. ~₹1–2L.
Month 4–8
Ecocert Organic & Natural + PETA Cruelty-Free. These unlock premium positioning and export credibility. Phool's exact template. ~₹2–3L.
Month 8–14
IEC + APEDA for export. WHO-GMP + Free Sale Certificate for US/EU/UAE markets. Full export readiness. ~₹3–5L total.
