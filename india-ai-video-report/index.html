Section 03 — Face Accuracy Analysis

Face Accuracy: What Works, What Looks Weird, and How to Fix It

This is the most honest section of this report. Current diffusion models produce faces that are ~80–92% accurate across neutral expressions, but degrade on extreme poses and motions. Understanding why — and the fixes — is critical to building a quality product.

✓

What looks natural (works well)

Neutral talking-head videos · straight-on angle
Gentle expressions (smile, slight eyebrow raise)
Slow to medium-pace dance with small head movement
Consistent lighting matching training photos
Fantasy / animal scenes (no identity to match)
Profile shots if training set included profile images
⚠

What looks "weird" (known issues)

Wide-open mouth (screaming, laughing hard) — teeth artifacts
Fear/shock expression — eyes go "uncanny valley"
Fast head turns > 45° — face warps momentarily
Skin tone mismatches under harsh/coloured lighting
Hands near face — collision artifacts
Multi-person scenes — wrong face applied to wrong body

Why Face Accuracy Degrades — Root Causes

IssueRoot CauseCurrent AccuracyFix
Extreme expressionsLoRA/InstantID was trained on neutral-to-mild expression photos; extreme poses outside training distribution~55–70%Include extreme expression photos in training set; GFPGAN face restoration post-pass
Fast head rotation (>45°)Video generation models process frames semi-independently; large pose changes exceed continuity model capacity~60–75%Temporal smoothing; face-specific ControlNet; optical flow loss
Skin tone under lightingIndian skin tones underrepresented in base model training → biased response to unfamiliar lighting~70–80%Fine-tune base model on Indian face dataset (IMFDB); use lighting-diverse training photos
Teeth / open mouthDiffusion models generate teeth probabilistically; high entropy region = inconsistent generation~50–65%CodeFormer inpainting on mouth region; LatentSync overrides mouth area anyway during lip sync
Temporal flickerFrame-to-frame independence in base video models; face details regenerated each framePresent in ~30% of clipsOptical flow warp; FILM frame interpolation; temporal consistency loss during training

The Fix Pipeline for Face Accuracy

1
Quality training photos

Require users to upload: front, 3/4 left, 3/4 right, slight smile, slight eyebrow raise, under natural light. 15–20 photos minimum. Auto-reject blurry, low-light, masked, or sunglassed photos via quality classifier.

2
Diverse lighting regularisation in LoRA

During LoRA training, include regularisation images of Indian faces under varied lighting (indoor yellow, outdoor, shade). Prevents skin tone drift under different scene lighting. Add 20–30 Indian face reg images from IMFDB.

3
GFPGAN / CodeFormer face restoration pass

After video generation, run a per-frame face restoration pass (GFPGAN v1.4 or CodeFormer). Fixes: blurry details, teeth artifacts, skin tone inconsistency. ~2–4 min on A100 for 30s video. Significant quality improvement for minimal extra cost (~₹3/video).

4
Temporal smoothing with FILM

Google FILM (Frame Interpolation for Large Motion) inserted between frames reduces flicker. Smooths face transitions during head turns. Run on RTX 3090 (16 GB VRAM), fast (~1 min/30s video).

5
LatentSync overrides mouth region (for talking videos)

If voice/speech is added, LatentSync's lip sync completely regenerates the mouth region synchronised to audio. This actually solves teeth/mouth artifacts automatically — the lip sync model learns correct mouth shapes from audio features.

6
User instruction: "Avoid extreme expressions in reference videos"

UI-level guidance: "For best results, upload reference videos with moderate expressions. Screaming/horror expressions will have lower face accuracy." Set expectations; give quality score preview before full generation.

Section 15 — Risks

Risk Register & Mitigations

⚑ IT Rules 2025 Non-compliance

Failing to inject AI labels or traceability IDs triggers government action. Mitigation: Build mandatory post-processing compliance step from Day 1; no video leaves pipeline without label + UUID.

⚑ Indian Face Quality Gap

Base models show Indian faces only ~5% on neutral prompts. Mitigation: Fine-tune on IMFDB + FairFace Indian subset in Q2. Becomes a defensible moat.

⚑ Face "Weird Expression" Churn

Users may churn when dance transfer produces uncanny expressions. Mitigation: GFPGAN + FILM fix pipeline; quality score preview before full generation; set clear expectations in UI for extreme expressions.

⚑ GPU Cost Escalation

At 10K users, infra = ₹55–80L/mo on owned hardware. Mitigation: Start on E2E spot (no capex). Transition to reserved at 1K users. Buy hardware at 5K+ users.

⚑ Model Obsolescence

Wan 2.1 superseded within 12 months. Mitigation: Model-agnostic orchestration layer. Swapping models = config change only.

⚑ Deepfake Misuse
