Understanding the Services

What Each Service Does — In Simple Terms

No jargon. Here is what each service means for AP Police operations.

Vulnerability Testing (VAPT)

Think: Security audit of a police station

Just like you would inspect a police station for weak locks, broken CCTV, or unguarded entry points — VAPT checks all police websites and apps for security weaknesses that hackers could exploit. We find the weak points before criminals do.

Dark Web Monitoring

Think: Informer network in the criminal underworld

The dark web is where criminals trade stolen data, passwords, and hacking tools. Our AI monitors these hidden networks 24/7 — like having informers in every criminal gang. If AP Police data appears on the dark web, we alert you within 5 minutes.

RASP (Real-time App Protection)

Think: Armed guard inside the building, not just at the gate

Traditional security (firewalls) is like posting guards at the gate. RASP puts a guard inside every room. If a hacker gets past the gate, RASP stops them at the point of attack — blocking the hack in real-time, from inside the application.

Cyber Forensics

Think: Crime scene investigation, but for computers

When a cyber crime occurs, we preserve digital evidence, trace the attacker's steps, and build a timeline — exactly like forensic teams at a crime scene. Our reports are court-admissible and ready for FIR documentation.

Ethical Hacking

Think: Mock drill to test your defences

Just like police conduct mock drills to test emergency response, ethical hackers attempt to break into your systems using the same techniques real criminals use — but safely. We find what a real attacker would find, and fix it before they can.

Incident Response (2-Hour SLA)

Think: Emergency response team for cyber attacks

When a cyber attack happens, every minute counts. Our team responds within 2 hours, contains the breach, preserves evidence, and files the mandatory CERT-In notification within the 6-hour government deadline. Like a QRT for cyber emergencies.

Employee Security Training

Think: Training constables to spot fake ID cards

91% of cyber attacks start with a phishing email. We train AP Police staff to recognise fake emails, WhatsApp messages, and scam links — in Hindi, Telugu, and English. Using realistic Indian scenarios like fake GST notices and UPI requests.

SOC-as-a-Service

Think: 24/7 control room monitoring all CCTV feeds

Just like a police control room monitors CCTV across the city, our Security Operations Centre monitors all AP Police digital systems 24/7, detecting suspicious activity and responding immediately.
